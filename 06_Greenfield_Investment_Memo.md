We recommend an **₹800–1,200 crore equity commitment** into a greenfield integrated township of 75–120 acres in NCR's eastern corridor (Indirapuram / Crossing Republik / Raj Nagar Extension / Greater Noida), delivering 8–12 million sqft of mixed-use development over a 7–10 year horizon.

**The opportunity:** NCR's eastern corridor (Ghaziabad-Noida axis) is home to 25M+ people with zero institutional-grade integrated townships. Jewar Airport (Dec 2027), RRTS (operational), and Noida Metro extension are creating infrastructure-driven price discovery comparable to Gurugram's 2010–2015 growth cycle. **The approach:** Acquire 75–120 acres of contiguous land at ₹1,500–2,500/sqft, master-plan a mixed-use township (60% residential, 15% retail, 15% office, 10% plotted/hospitality), develop in 4–5 phases over 7–10 years, and extract value through phased price discovery, commercial asset stabilization, and township brand premium.

## 2. INVESTMENT THESIS

### Core Thesis
Build NCR's first institutional-grade integrated township in the eastern corridor — where 25M people, ₹1,500–2,500/sqft land, Jewar Airport, and zero competition from branded townships create a once-in-a-decade city-creation opportunity yielding 22%+ net IRR with 2.5x+ MOIC.

### Five Pillars

1. **White Space — Zero Competition:** Gurugram has DLF Golf Links, Noida has Jaypee Greens (failed). Eastern NCR has nothing. The corridor's 12M+ households have no institutional township alternative. First-mover creates the category.

2. **Infrastructure Convergence:** Jewar Airport + RRTS + Metro Extension + EPE — all converging in 2026–2028 window. Historical pattern: Gurugram land prices 5x'd in 5 years after Metro + NH-8 improvements (2007–2012). We see similar trajectory for eastern corridor.

3. **Phased Value Creation:** Launch Phase 1 at ₹5,500/sqft → deliver amenities → launch Phase 2 at ₹6,500/sqft → demonstrate community → Phase 3 at ₹8,000/sqft → Phase 4 at ₹10,000+/sqft. **60–80% price appreciation is earned through execution, not market beta.**

4. **Mixed-Use Diversification:** Commercial component (2.5–3.0M sqft office + retail) generates ₹100–150 crore NOI at stabilization. This is an embedded yield asset that provides (a) downside protection, (b) REIT exit optionality, and (c) enhances residential desirability.

5. **FSI Arbitrage:** UP township policy grants 3.5–4.0 FSI (vs. 2.5 for standalone). On 100 acres, this translates to 10–12M sqft saleable vs. 7–8M sqft for standalone development — 40% more revenue on same land cost.

## 7. RISKS & MITIGATION

| # | Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|------|------------|--------|------------|
| 1 | **Land title/litigation** — UP real estate has history of disputed titles, encroachment, litigation | High | High | Triple-verification title DD; title insurance; avoid disputed parcels; escrow structure for land payment |
| 2 | **Execution risk** — 10M sqft over 7–10 years requires elite execution capability | High | High | Partner with proven township developer (Prestige/Godrej/Lodha); independent PMC; phased go/no-go gates |
| 3 | **Regulatory risk** — UP approval process is slow (24–36 months for large township) | High | Medium | Begin approval process during DD; political risk assessment; engage local consultants; buffer 12 months in plan |
| 4 | **Demand risk** — eastern NCR is price-sensitive; ₹8,000+/sqft may not sustain | Medium | Medium | Phase 1 at affordable pricing to prove demand; escalation tied to actual sales velocity |
| 5 | **Market cycle risk** — 7–10 year exposure spans inevitable downcycles | Medium | Medium | Phase-gate model — don't launch next phase unless 60% of current phase sold; maintain 12-month cash buffer |
| 6 | **Cash flow mismatch** — RERA escrow limits use of collections; construction debt may be constrained | Medium | Medium | Match debt tenure to phase timeline; negotiate working capital limits; pre-sales fund majority of construction |
| 7 | **Competitive entry** — Prestige, Godrej may directly enter eastern NCR | Low | High | First-mover advantage; 24+ month lead time; lock in best land parcel in corridor |

## 9. EXIT STRATEGY

### 9.1 Primary Exit: Phase-wise Unit Sell-Down (60% probability)
- Each phase generates returns independently
- Phase 1–2 returns recycled into Phase 3–4
- Target: 90% sold by OC date; 100% within 6 months post-OC
- **Advantage:** No dependency on single exit event; cash flows distributed over time
- **IRR impact:** 22–26% (base case)

### 9.2 Secondary Exit: Commercial Asset Sale (20% probability)
- At stabilization (Year 6–8), office + retail portfolio generates ₹80–120 Cr NOI
- Sale to REIT or institutional buyer at 8–8.5% cap rate = ₹940–1,500 Cr
- **Advantage:** Single-event crystallization of commercial value
- **IRR impact:** Enhances blended IRR by 200–300bps

### 9.3 Tertiary Exit: Platform Strategic Sale (10% probability)
- Sell entire township platform (HoldCo + land bank + pipeline) to a tier-1 developer
- Buyers: Prestige (NCR entry), Godrej (scale), Lodha (replication of Palava model)
- Valuation: NAV + 15–20% control premium
- Timeline: Year 5–6

### 9.4 Developer Buyback (10% probability)
- Pre-agreed put option with developer partner at 18% IRR compounding
- Triggered if market conditions deteriorate or fund requires early exit
- Secured by developer's share of project assets + personal guarantees
