## CORE THESIS

---

## WHY NOW

### Macro Tailwinds
- **India GCC explosion:** India hosts 1,700+ GCCs (up from 1,100 in 2020) — each GCC requires 50,000–500,000 sqft of Grade A space. NCR is #2 GCC market after Bengaluru
- **Office absorption at all-time high:** India absorbed 72M sqft in CY2025 — NCR contributed 18M sqft (25% share), growing at 22% YoY
- **Rental growth cycle:** Gurugram Grade A rentals at ₹95–140/sqft/month — 15–20% above 2019 levels, still 30–40% below Mumbai BKC and 60% below Singapore CBD
- **REIT maturity:** India REIT market at ₹80,000 crore market cap; Embassy, Mindspace, Brookfield REIT trading at 7–8% yield — clear exit pathway
- **FDI liberalization:** 100% FDI in commercial real estate under automatic route

### NCR-Specific Catalysts
- **Gurugram as India's corporate HQ:** 300+ Fortune 500 companies; Goldman Sachs, American Express, Google, Microsoft expanding
- **Supply-demand mismatch:** Gurugram Grade A vacancy at 8–10% (healthy); new supply pipeline insufficient to meet 15M+ sqft annual demand
- **Dwarka Expressway opening:** New commercial corridor with 40% lower land costs vs. Golf Course Road — early-mover advantage
- **Metro connectivity:** Yellow Line extension to Cybercity, Rapid Metro operational — improving last-mile
- **Flex space demand:** 25–30% of new leasing is flex/managed office — premium on well-located, well-designed assets

## KEY RISKS

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| **GCC demand slowdown** — global recession, AI-driven headcount reduction | HIGH | Diversify tenant mix (30% domestic corporates); lock in long-term leases with committed rent escalations |
| **Oversupply in new corridors** (Dwarka Expressway, New Gurugram) | MEDIUM | Focus on established micro-markets (Golf Course Ext., Cybercity); avoid speculative development in untested corridors |
| **Interest rate risk** — rising rates compress cap rates, increase debt cost | MEDIUM | Lock in fixed-rate debt for 3–5 years; structure with 55% max LTV; natural hedge via rental escalations (5% p.a.) |
