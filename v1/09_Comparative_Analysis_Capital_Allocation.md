## 2. RISK-ADJUSTED RANKING

### Rank #1: BROWNFIELD (Developer Capital Infusion) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Score: 92/100**

- **Why #1:** Highest risk-adjusted return. 20% net IRR in 3.2 years = best IRR per unit time. Lowest execution risk (known projects, known costs). Structural downside protection (18% pref, 1.3x collateral, personal guarantees). Portfolio effect across 4–6 projects absorbs individual project failure.
- **The edge:** In a market with 300+ eligible deals, we pick the best 4–6. Information advantage is real — RERA data, construction progress, sales velocity are all verifiable.
- **Concern:** Smaller MOIC (1.9x) limits absolute profit; requires re-deployment for fund-level returns.

### Rank #2: RESIDENTIAL (Mid-Income, Spillover Markets) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Score: 85/100**

- **Why #2:** Sweet spot of demand (₹30–55L units = deepest pool), margin (55–65% gross), and scalability (6–8 projects). Infrastructure catalysts (Jewar, RRTS) are free optionality — underwrite without them. Pre-sales de-risk capital before construction.
- **The edge:** No institutional capital in Sonipat/Panipat corridor. Brand premium in unbranded markets is 10–15%. Repeatable product = operating leverage.
- **Concern:** Developer partner quality in Tier-2 NCR; infrastructure delays could slow price discovery.

### Rank #3: COMMERCIAL (Grade A Office, Gurugram) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Score: 82/100**

- **Why #3:** Most defensive asset class — 8.5%+ stabilized yield, 5% contractual escalation, GCC tenants with 90%+ renewal rates. REIT exit is mature and liquid. Positive leverage from Day 1. India REIT market is supply-constrained.
- **The edge:** Development component (40% allocation) creates 70–100% value on cost. Blended portfolio offers income + growth.
- **Concern:** Largest ticket (₹1,000–1,500 Cr) = highest concentration. Cap rate expansion in rising rate environment is the primary risk. Longest payback (56 months).

### Rank #4: GREENFIELD (Integrated Township) ⭐⭐⭐
**Score: 72/100**

- **Why #4:** Highest absolute return potential (22% net IRR, 2.2x MOIC) but offset by highest execution risk, longest duration (7–10 years), and significant regulatory/land title risk in UP. Category-creation opportunity but with commensurate complexity.
- **The edge:** Only institutional township in eastern NCR — defining a new market. Commercial component provides downside floor. FSI bonus creates structural cost advantage.
- **Concern:** UP land title risk is real. 7–10 year commitment spans market cycles. Developer partner with township capability is hard to find in NCR. Regulatory timeline is unpredictable.

## 4. STRATEGIC RATIONALE FOR MULTI-STRATEGY APPROACH

### 4.1 Why Not Concentrate?

**Single-strategy funds underperform in India RE because:**
- Market cycles are unpredictable — residential crashed 2015–2020 while commercial boomed
- Regulatory shocks are sector-specific — demonetization hit residential, not commercial
- Exit windows are different — REIT (commercial) vs. unit sales (residential) vs. structured (brownfield)
- Duration mismatch — LPs want DPI by Year 4; only brownfield delivers this as a standalone

### 4.2 Portfolio Synergies

| Synergy | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Brownfield → Residential pipeline** | Best brownfield developers become residential platform partners |
| **Residential → Greenfield land bank** | Residential micro-market knowledge informs township site selection |
| **Commercial income → Fund operations** | Stabilized commercial yield covers fund-level expenses during J-curve |
| **Cross-collateral intelligence** | Market data from 15+ projects across NCR provides unmatched insight for pricing, timing, and exits |

### 4.3 Risk Diversification Matrix

| Risk Event | Residential | Commercial | Greenfield | Brownfield | Portfolio Impact |
|-----------|-------------|------------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Interest rate +300bps | ❌ High | ⚠️ Medium | ❌ High | ⚠️ Low | Moderate — 40% in commercial (income hedge) |
| NCR demand crash (-30%) | ❌ High | ⚠️ Low | ❌ High | ⚠️ Medium | Moderate — brownfield has pref protection |
| RERA tightening | ⚠️ Medium | ✅ Low | ⚠️ High | ⚠️ Medium | Low — commercial unaffected |
