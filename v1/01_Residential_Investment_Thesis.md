## CORE THESIS

> **Invest ₹500–700 crore in a scalable mid-income residential platform targeting NCR spillover markets (Sonipat, Panipat, Sohna, Greater Noida West) where structural demand-supply mismatch, RERA-driven market consolidation, and infrastructure-led price discovery create a compelling risk-adjusted return opportunity at 20%+ net IRR.**

---

## WHY NOW

### Macro Tailwinds
- **India's housing shortage:** ~10 million urban units deficit; government targeting "Housing for All" with ₹79,000 crore PMAY allocation (FY26 Budget)
- **Interest rate environment:** RBI repo rate at 6.00% (April 2026), home loan rates at 8.25–8.75% — lowest in 3 years, driving affordability
- **Credit availability:** Banks' exposure to housing up 16% YoY; NHBs disbursing aggressively in ₹25–75 lakh ticket segment
- **Demographics:** 12–15 million annual urban migration; median age 28 — peak household formation cohort

### NCR-Specific Catalysts
- **Infrastructure acceleration:** RRTS Delhi-Meerut (operational), Jewar Airport (Phase 1: Dec 2027), Delhi-Sonipat RRTS (approved), KMP Expressway fully operational
- **Price correction complete:** NCR residential prices corrected 15–25% in real terms (2016–2022); current prices at 2015 levels in many micro-markets — floor established
- **RERA clean-up:** 60%+ unorganized developers exited post-RERA/NBFC crisis; market share consolidating to top-20 players
- **Spillover economics:** Gurugram/Noida core markets at ₹12,000–18,000/sqft; spillover markets at ₹3,500–6,000/sqft — 3–4x affordability advantage
- **Absorption recovery:** NCR residential sales volume up 28% YoY (FY25); spillover markets outperforming at 35%+ growth

## KEY VALUE DRIVERS

| Driver | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Land acquisition at ₹800–1,200/sqft** in Sonipat/Panipat vs. ₹4,000–8,000/sqft in core NCR | 60–70% gross margin headroom |
| **Construction cost arbitrage** — ₹1,800–2,200/sqft in Tier-2 vs. ₹2,500–3,000/sqft in core NCR | 15–20% cost advantage |
| **RERA-driven consolidation** — partner with top-2 local developer or build own branded platform | Category-defining positioning |
| **Pre-sales velocity** — target 60–70% pre-sales before construction completion | De-risks capital, accelerates IRR |
| **Infrastructure-led repricing** — RRTS/Jewar proximity drives 30–50% price appreciation over hold period | Equity upside independent of market beta |
| **Ticket-size sweet spot** — ₹30–55 lakh units = deepest demand pool, lowest inventory risk | 85%+ end-user demand (minimal speculative) |

---

## EDGE / COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE

1. **Capital scarcity premium:** Limited institutional capital in Tier-2 NCR; developers starved for equity → negotiate preferred returns, co-invest structures, profit-sharing with downside protection
2. **Execution flywheel:** Standardized product (2BHK/3BHK), repeatable across 4–6 micro-markets; Eldeco-style platform economics (₹100 crore per project × 6–8 projects)
3. **Brand premium in unbranded markets:** Institutional-grade product in markets dominated by unbranded developers commands 10–15% price premium
4. **Exit optionality:** Multiple exit paths — sell-down to retail, developer buyback, platform-level strategic sale, REIT-readiness for rental component

---

## KEY RISKS

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| **Infrastructure delay** (Jewar/RRTS pushback by 2–3 years) | HIGH | Underwrite returns assuming NO infrastructure premium; infrastructure upside = free optionality |
| **Demand concentration risk** — single geography, single segment | MEDIUM | Diversify across 4+ micro-markets within NCR; mix of plotted + apartment product |
| **Developer execution risk** — local partners may lack institutional governance | MEDIUM | Board seats, milestone-linked capital drawdowns, independent PMC oversight, RERA escrow compliance |
