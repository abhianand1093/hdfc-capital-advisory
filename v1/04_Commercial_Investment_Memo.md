## 2. INVESTMENT THESIS

### Core Thesis
Gurugram's Grade A office market offers the rare combination of 8.5%+ yields (vs. 5–6% in developed markets), structural demand growth (GCCs + domestic tech), and REIT exit maturity — making it one of the highest risk-adjusted return opportunities in Asia-Pacific commercial real estate.

### Five Pillars

1. **GCC Structural Demand:** India's GCC sector adding 3M sqft/quarter in NCR alone. MNC occupiers require IGBC/LEED-certified, campus-style office with 150+ car parks/100K sqft, floor plates >25,000 sqft, and 5-star lobby specifications. Supply of this quality is constrained to <15% of Gurugram stock.

2. **Yield + Growth:** Stabilized Grade A assets in Gurugram yield 8.5–9.5% with 5% contractual escalation — among the highest institutional-grade yields globally. Combined with 10–15% rental growth in prime corridors, total return potential is 18–22%.

3. **REIT Exit Pathway:** India's three listed REITs (Embassy, Mindspace, Brookfield) are actively acquiring NCR assets. Brookfield REIT's NCR portfolio at ₹16,000 crore AUM. REIT demand for quality NCR assets exceeds supply. Cap rate compression from 9% (entry) to 7.5–8% (exit) is achievable.

4. **Development Margin:** Build-to-lease at ₹5,500–6,500/sqft (land + construction + approvals) in Golf Course Extension / Dwarka Expressway. Stabilized value at ₹12,000–16,000/sqft (based on 7.75% cap rate on ₹100–120/sqft/month rent). **70–100% value creation on development component.**

5. **Leverage Positive Carry:** PSU bank LRD at 8.5–9.0% on assets yielding 8.5–9.5%. With 55% LTV, equity yield is 9–11% from Day 1 on stabilized assets — **self-funding while waiting for cap rate compression.**

## 9. EXIT STRATEGY

### 9.1 Primary Exit: REIT Contribution (50% probability)
- Contribute portfolio (3–4 assets, 4–5M sqft, ₹155+ Cr NOI) to existing India REIT
- Embassy, Mindspace, or Brookfield REIT actively acquiring NCR assets
- Pricing: 7.5–8.0% cap rate = ₹1,940–2,070 Cr portfolio value
- Transaction cost: 1.5–2% (legal, due diligence, stamp duty exemption for REIT)
- Timeline: Year 5–6

### 9.2 Secondary Exit: Institutional Sale (30% probability)
- Sell to sovereign wealth fund (GIC, ADIA, PIF), pension fund (CPPIB, CDPQ), or insurance company (LIC, SBI Life)
- Premium for stabilized, REIT-eligible portfolio with ESG certification
- Pricing: 7.75–8.25% cap rate (slight discount to REIT for block transaction)
- Timeline: Year 5–7

### 9.3 Tertiary Exit: Asset-Level Sale (15% probability)
- Sell individual assets to developers (DLF, Godrej) or HNI family offices
- Higher cap rate (8.5–9%) but faster execution, no REIT regulatory dependency
- Timeline: Year 4–6

### 9.4 Optionality: Sponsor REIT IPO
- If portfolio reaches ₹200+ Cr NOI, consider sponsoring dedicated REIT
- Minimum size: ₹3,000 Cr AUM for REIT listing
- 5% probability — requires additional acquisitions
